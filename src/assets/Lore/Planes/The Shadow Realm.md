Not much is known about the shadow realm by most, other than it is a prison for evil people that once tried to take over the world. To the Elven mages that created it though, they know the Shadow Realm to be an entirely subterranean world with large sets of interconnected tunnels. 

Since its creation though, it has changed a great deal. At the turn of the era to [[The Age of Darkness]] a group of heroes visited the Shadow Realm to find that its inhabitants had changed nearly as much as the realm itself. Their accounts revealed that the realm was now wrought with rivers of blood pooling out of cave mouths into enormous lakes. Tunnels of worked stone led to numerous rooms that were often dedicated to profane rituals or prisons.

A unique breed of fiends obsessed with self-perfection took to the art of warping flesh, stealing it from others in order to graft onto themselves until they were no longer recognizable from the elves they once were. They would take creatures and "perfect" them by shaping them into horrifying monstrosities to do their bidding. Fleshwarping, as it was called, became a word powerful enough to send a shiver down your spine.

Little was explored during this time, but considering the nature of the creatures that emerged from within during wars at the turn of the era, its safe to assume they many regions within the Shadow Realm are twisted in their own unique ways.
