Tags: [[Su]], [[Scrying]]
You can project your sense of sight and hearing to any location you have line of sight to, initiating as a standard action, then maintaining it for a few minutes. You can move the location you view as though you were walking around in the area. You are still unable to view anything you do not have line of sight to from your original position (ie you cannot see around a giant pillar by projecting yourself to the side behind it). This basically makes eye in the sky function as extremely powerful senses of sight and hearing that require active concentration to maintain. While you do so, your body is vulnerable and [[stunned]]. When you stop scrying, you are [[drained]] 2.

With good environmental conditions, that means you can potentially see incredibly far distances. Clouds do not obscure your line of sight with this ability. Depending on your height relative to the area around you. Here's a handy chart that should give you a basic idea of how far you can see with specific elevations.

| <span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">Elevation (relative)</span> | <span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">Distance to Horizon</span> |
| ------------------------------------------------------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1 ft                                                               | 1 mile                                                            |
| 100 ft                                                             | 12 miles                                                          |
| 1000 ft                                                            | 38 miles                                                          |
| 1 mile                                                             | 90 miles                                                          |
| 6 miles                                                            | 220 miles                                                         |
6 miles was chosen as the final threshold since that's around the average most commercial airlines fly, and isn't far off from the tallest mountain peaks IRL. One mile further and you are encroaching upon the stratosphere, which is where common atmospheric conditions we're used to on the surface get weird (extremely low air density making it hard to breathe, wild variations in temperature, high UV radiation, ie not recommended to go higher).
