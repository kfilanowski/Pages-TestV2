You can visibly distinguish particularly novel pieces of literature. This manifests as a golden glow that outlines the outside of the book. This light appears over all one of a kind written works, but the glow is more vibrant the more esoteric the content within. Something like the diary cataloguing the daily life of a child would have a very dim glow. A scroll containing details about a saucy love affair shared between standing leaders of neighboring nations may glow brightly. A tome containing the recipe to an immortality elixir would be <span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">blindingly bright</span>. This brightness recedes when the tome is opened, allowing the text to be read normally.

You can also perform a 1 minute ritual in order to send such tomes directly to [[The Celestial Archive]]. During this time, it appears as though the pages of the work are all falling out of it, spiraling one by one into a shining portal. As such, it leaves a lot to be desired as a stealth operation. Particularly bright tomes are encouraged and rewarded while literature deemed unimportant will be punished.

- For sending unlit or dim tomes, you are punished with [[Advantage|disadvantage]] on your next [[+Knowledge]] check of significance (ie rolling something just to get rid of it is discouraged).
- For sending lit tomes, you are granted a [[Advantage|reroll]], usable at any time for any type of knowledge check.
- For sending the particularly rare <span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">blinding tomes</span>, you can [[Innate Technique|innately cast]] the [[sending]] spell once to ask Celeste for a piece of esoteric knowledge. She responds with only evidence and fact-based knowledge gathered over many centuries. Esoteric knowledge can be practically anything, and is still limited by her own knowledge. After all, if she knew everything, you wouldn't be sending her these books! If she believes she cannot adequately answer your question, she will say so, and grant you the opportunity to ask another.
