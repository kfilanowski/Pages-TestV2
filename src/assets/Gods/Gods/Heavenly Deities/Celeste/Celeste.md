The Celestial Archive, often shortened to Celeste, is the goddess of hoarding knowledge. To tasks are simple: The accumulation of the knowledge of all realms, and its safeguard within her archive. She encourages her followers to dive into the depths of the sea to dredge up artifacts, or explore remains of destroyed cities to find a single book. Though her followers are often encouraged to pursue ancient and lost knowledge, they are also encouraged to relinquish it to her. Because of this, she has a mixed reputation amongst academics and researchers as they do not appreciate her taking their tomes away. In particular, her reputation amongst elves is very antagonistic, as they tend to have much more organized and robust practices when it comes to preserving knowledge. 

But what is the point of the accumulation of knowledge without purpose? What Celeste is actually more interested in is shaping the direction of humanity through knowledge. This only creates more questions. Depending on the age, her answer may differ: To see what happens when the world order changes to favor a new being or perhaps to witness the how far mortals can fall and bounce back from. 

The intrinsic promise behind her worship is that when her followers give knowledge to her, they receive knowledge in return. The knowledge she gives is very selective, meant to nudge them towards a particular path she thinks is most interesting, or perhaps the path she believes will yield the most new knowledge far in the future. In this sense, she treats all of existence as though it were one big experiment where the knowledge she holds is the only control she can exert over it.

Celeste is depicted as a young women with dull gray skin that has the consistency of paper. She hovers just above the ground while she reads, wearing robes that cover most of her body. While animated, her feet pop out of the bottom of the robes so she can walk around. Her head and neck are covered in what looks like loose sheets of paper resembling messy medium length hair. There is black text written on her robes in hair in tasteful patterns that vary depending on when she is seen.
