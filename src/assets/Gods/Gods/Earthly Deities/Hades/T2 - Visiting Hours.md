You gain the ability to bring back the dead!...Temporarily at least. With a 1 minute ritual you can allow those grieving to say one last goodbye. You have to unambiguously identify a creature that you knew that has died within the past ten years while adjacent to the remains with a keepsake of the creature. Only a small portion of the remains is necessary. 

If the creature is willing to return (the creature knows who is bringing them back and who is visiting to make this decision), then a makeshift body that resembles what they looked like in life slowly materializes over the cast time. This does not disturb the remains. The creature is a simulacrum of its former self with a sandy texture to it that slowly crumbles away. A creature knowledgeable of the spell can identify a creature created by this effect with a DC 10 [[+Religion]] check. The creature can walk and talk for 24 hours before it completely fades away.

Due to the limited nature of the copy, if the creature suffers 10 points of damage, they immediately crumble and turn to dust, leaving behind the keepsake used in to create the copy.  They cannot [[Attack]], use [[Su]] abilities or cast [[Spell|spells]].

This effect is typically used after soldiers die to give loved ones a chance to say goodbye to their family and friends. It can also be used to collect information that otherwise would have been lost.

An individual can only be the target of this effect once.
