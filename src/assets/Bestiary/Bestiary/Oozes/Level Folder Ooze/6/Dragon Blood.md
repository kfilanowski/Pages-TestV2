[[Engulf]] - Every turn engulfed, the trapped creature takes 10 necrotic damage.
<span style="color:rgb(192, 0, 0)">Hydragon Blood</span> - Dragon blood functions as though it were several different creatures all attached to each other. This should be represented through 1 massive sized creature token as the head, and then 3 smaller medium sized tokens that each represent one of its ravenous pseudopods. 
They still all act on the same turn, but each of the pseudopods gets its own [[simple attack]] each round. All of the lesser "heads" must remain within 30 ft of the main head. The lesser heads [[threaten]], but dragon blood can only make a single AoO against a creature, regardless of how many heads threaten a single creature. Similarly, AoE effects cannot double (or quadruple) up on damage when targeting multiple heads. Effects like [[Maneuver Versatility|MV]] that can only be used 1/rnd are still the case etc.
<span style="color:rgb(192, 0, 0)">Life Blood</span> - Whenever dragon blood is subject to a [[healing]] effect, the main head makes an [[AoO]] against a random creature it threatens.
<span style="color:rgb(192, 0, 0)">Festering Blood</span> - Functions as [[bleeding]] for 2d6 damage. While bleeding, all [[healing]] is halved, and other half is redirected to the dragon blood. The DC to remove the bleed is 22.

## <span style="color:rgb(247, 138, 255)">Weaknesses/Deep Lore</span>

<span style="color:rgb(247, 138, 255)">Indigestion</span> - Dragon blood is much better at digesting the meat than anything else. It can be convinced, very forcefully, to eat anything. Objects roughly the size of a person are not particularly effective due to the large size of the ooze. An object must be roughly 10x10x10 ft before it has any noticeable effects.

Such objects when thrust/dropped into the bloods amorphous body deal 10 [[true damage]]. Extremely large objects, like a large shipping vessel, deal 20 true damage and completely occupy one of dragon blood's lesser heads.
<span style="color:rgb(247, 138, 255)">Assimilation</span> - Whenever dragon blood randomly targets an enemy, it instinctually attacks any [[Dragons]] first.
