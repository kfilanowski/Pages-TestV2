Most of the time, gravity should not be much of an issue, but in particularly strange scenarios gravity can change to be more or less intense. This section should help you navigate such scenarios.

<span style="color:rgb(192, 0, 0)">Low Gravity</span> 
Low gravity lets creatures move vertically a lot more freely. It no longer requires a swift action in order to take the [[Jump]] action and creatures can automatically jump as far and as high as a creature's [[Land Speed]]. Low gravity also negates [[Fall Damage]].

Whenever you force a creature to move in low gravity, such as when you [[Shove]] a creature, the distance you move them is increased by 10 feet.

<span style="color:rgb(192, 0, 0)">No Gravity</span>
Low gravity can be very complicated. Many features of the game assume gravity, y'know, exists. The combination of gravity and friction are the foundation of how we understand basic physics. In the absence of gravity, all momentum is practically infinite. If you push yourself, or a creature pushes you, off of a surface, you will continue to move in that direction until acted upon by another force. This can potentially lead to scenarios where creatures unintentionally float off into directions much further than they would like.

Actions that require deliberate force, such as a [[Shove]] attack, also have an equal an opposite effect on the shover. All forced movement is increased by 20 ft. This forced movement becomes a [[Persistent]] effect that triggers each round without another force at play.

All [[Land Speed]] is changed to 30 ft. If you move in a direction, you require anchors in order move in anything other than a straight line. Some locations may still allow you to use [[Climb]] speeds. [[Fly]] speeds require air in order to function and thus do not work in the vacuum of space. Zero gravity in aquatic scenarios typically won't have an impact unless it is activated near the water's surface, but otherwise, [[Swim]] speeds should function as normal.

<span style="color:rgb(192, 0, 0)">High Gravity</span>
High gravity restricts movement and affects how much you can lift. Creature's with low [[STR]] will be more impeded than others. All creatures with movement impeded by high gravity are [[Grounded]].

<span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">Tier 1 </span>- Creatures with -1 [[STR]] or less have all of their speeds halved. All creatures can only lift/manipulate 50% as much.
<span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">Tier 2 </span>- Creatures with +1 STR or less have all their speeds halved. Creatures with -1 or less STR must roll a DC 10 STR [[Ability Check]] each round to move at all. All creatures can only lift/manipulate a 20% as much.
<span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">Tier 3</span> - Creatures with +3 STR or less have all of their speeds halved. Creatures with -1 or less STR must roll a DC 15 STR ability check each round to move at all. Creatures with +1 or less must roll a DC 10 check. All creatures can only lift/manipulate 10% as much.

The distance of all forced movement is halved unless the creature initiating it is unaffected by the speed penalty of the high gravity. Yes I understand this means muscular psionics are better at telekinesis in high gravity. Why don't YOU try and lift a staff when suddenly it weighs 10x as much.

You can create more extreme scenarios by continuing to increase these numbers in the same linear fashion, but tier 3 is already crippling almost all creatures.
