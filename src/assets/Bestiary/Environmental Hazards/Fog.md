Fog, mist, dust, smoke and other dense air particles with occasionally fill or pepper the battlefield, obscuring vision. The extent to which varies. I have simplified the condition by just naming it fog.

<span style="color:rgb(192, 0, 0)">Solid Fog</span> - Solid fog is the most common form of fog created by creature's special abilities. Solid fog gives creatures beyond 5 ft [[Total Concealment]]. Even adjacent creatures still have [[Partial Concealment]].

<span style="color:rgb(192, 0, 0)">Standard Fog</span> - Standard fog is generally the more common environmental effect. Rather than a specific range, I will separate categories with specific penalties assigned to them. You can assign a distance, like 120 ft, or 60 or 30 on particularly foggy days.
I will use 120 ft as my example.

<span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">First Increment (0-120 ft) </span>- The fog obscures vision, applying a -4 penalty to [[+Perception]].
<span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">Second Increment (125-240ft) </span>- People can easily fade away in the fog. Grants [[Partial Concealment]].
<span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">Third Increment (245+ ft)</span> - Nothing beyond this can be seen. Grants [[Total Concealment]].
