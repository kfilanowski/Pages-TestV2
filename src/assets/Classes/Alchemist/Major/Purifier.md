## <span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">1 - Major: Purification</span>
You fix and heal, but not in the traditional sense. Your area of expertise is in reversing the irreversible. It is not all too uncommon to find individuals who are victims of a forced [[Reincarnate]]  from [[Frogg Saran]] or that are being changed by some kind of alien mutation or profane corruption. You aim to turn back time to where the creature was healthy whole and themselves again...eventually.

Using 100g of materials and 1 minute of time...
- You can fashion a prosthetic limb or organ fitted to a specific creature's needs. When inserted or attached, the organ assumes normal functionality, albeit with some getting used to. Limbs inflict [[clumsy]] 1 and organs inflict [[sickened]] 1. These debuffs are considered [[Permanent Status]] and persist for 1 week. At the end of the week, the limbs are mostly indistinguishable from the originals, leaving only connecting scar tissue.
- You can also create a foil to the effects of a [[Styx Serum]], or other effects that remove memories or sense of self. A year comes back to them for each day that follows them drinking your memory potion.
- Anything reasonably close to these abilities in terms of power/effect
## <span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)"> 6 - Thesis</span>
Using a laboratory, 1,000g of materials and 1 minute of time...
- Limbs/organs you replace do not apply debuffs
- You can reverse the effects of a [[Reincarnate]] spell, returning the creature to its previous form.
- If a creature is corrupted but not yet completely consumed by some malicious force, such as a creature part way through transforming into a fiend, you can reverse the direction of this corruption. Over time after administering your solution, the creature reverts back to its old self, assuming it does not continue to actively corrupt further.
- Anything reasonably close to these abilities in terms of power/effect

What counts as "corruption" is intentionally left vague, because alchemists are supposed to break rules, not be bound by them. In fantasy, any number of things can "corrupt" for greater power or as a consequence of associating with evil. You can counter the [[GM Magic]] if you manage to capture corrupted allies or save otherwise unwilling enemies.
## <span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">11 - Grand Discovery</span>
Using a laboratory, 10,000g, 1 rare materials and 1 minute of time you can restore a creature back from nearly anything short of death.

A rare material is again, intentionally vague, because its not an item that exists in the game, but is something that should exist in the world. These ingredients should be extracted from creatures that have incredible restorative properties, are immortal or something similar.
- A [[phoenix]] feather
- A shard from an [[ancient monolith]] 
- Blood from a [[Celestial]] or a powerful [[troll]]

By using a laboratory, 100,000g, 2 different rare materials and 2 weeks of active labor, you can create a clone of a creature you have a piece of (such as a blood sample, hair or a limb). This clone requires a full year to gestate before it grows into an adult.

The clone is inert until the original dies. When they do, they wake up in the body of the clone a few minutes later. Their max [[+HP]] is permanently reduced by 10 and they are [[Drained]] 20. Roll on the [[Resurrection Chart]]. Clones cannot be reborn as clones.
A creature trained in [[+Medicine]] that is giving treatment to a clone can discern the strangeness of this body with a DC equal to 10+The alchemist's [[Alchemy]] bonus.
