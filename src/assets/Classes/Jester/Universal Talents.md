## <span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">2 - It's Just a Prank Bro</span>
You can say anything you want to people, as long as its a joke. NPCs will not react negatively to anything you say, even if you call them a whore, a failure or a poopoo head. All you have to do is add a qualifying statement like "just kidding" or integrate it into a joke and all is forgiven. 
## <span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">2 - You Fool!</span>
You get a +10 bonus to [[Skill Check|skill checks]] and [[Ability Check|ability checks]] whenever you can reasonable use your incompetence as a reason for why you did something, like [[+Deception]] to say "you didn't mean it" or [[+Athletics]] to "accidentally" break something. 

Creatures other than your allies need to be present for you to gain this bonus.
## <span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">2 - Fooling Around</span>
Whether its talking nonsense after failing a [[+Monster Lore]] check or being overly dramatic about falling after failing to [[Balance]], you can turn it around. Whenever you miss a creature with an [[Attack]] or fail a [[Skill Check]]/[[Ability Check]], you can make it seem like it was all part of your plan to get a quick laugh out of creatures. 

As an immediate action, grant an ally witnessing your failure [[Advantage]] on their next d20 roll within the next minute. You can also pretend to fail at something to grant this advantage.

No matter the case, creatures other than your allies must be present to witness the tomfoolery and it always must be silly and dramatic, aka, something that will draw attention.

<span style="color:rgb(192, 0, 0)">Note:</span>
If you are not under duress, if there is no tension, you cannot fool around. This should not be read as "standard action grant advantage to every roll ever."
