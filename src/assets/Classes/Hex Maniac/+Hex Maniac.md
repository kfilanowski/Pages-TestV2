Hex maniacs are are mages that primarily utilize [[curse]] effects and [[alchemy]] in order to differentiate themselves from other mages. They can turn parts of creatures into useful and unusual potions or poisons, and they can inflict all kinds of colorful permanent debuffs on creatures.

## <span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">1 - Techniques</span>
You have [[Magical Specialization]].
You gain 2 technique points at level 1, then another 2 point every 2 levels after.
These technique points can be used on the [[Corruption]] and [[Alchemy]] trees.
## <span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">1 - Curse Mastery</span>
- You get the [[arcana]] skill with a +4 bonus. You can use arcana instead of medicine to [[Identify Affliction or Curse (P)|identify curse]]s.
- You can prepare any [[Technique Rules|technique]] with the [[curse]] tag, as long as you would normally be able to prepare techniques of that level (ie you can prepare [[Power of the Sun]] if you are level 3).
- You gain [[resistance]] to curse effects.
- Whenever you pass a save against a curse effect, you can redirect it back at the creature that it inflicted it upon you, who must save against their own curse. This costs an amount of [[mana]] equal to what was spent on the technique, or (1/2 the creature's [[LvL]]) for non-technique curses.
## <span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">1 - Cackle</span> [[Su]]
With an insult, sinister laugh or something to that effect, you can unnerve your foes. As a swift action, you can increase the [[DC]] or [[Caster Level|CL]] of the next spell you cast with the [[curse]] tag. If your target is a [[minion]], the creature gets no saving throw at all against cackle-enhanced curses.
## <span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">1 - Hex</span> [[Sp]]
You get access to unique curses, called hexes. Using a hex requires a standard action. All hexes have the [[curse]] tag, 30 ft range and cannot target the same creature more than once (ever). You get two right now!

<span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">Assurance</span> [[subtle]] - You can ask creatures to perform a task, including a time frame of 1 minute, 1 hour, 1 day, or 1 week. If they agree, they become cursed (will negates). When the time frame specified passes, the curse's debilitating effects activate. The creature gets [[Advantage|disadvantage]] on all [[+Saving Throw|saving throws]] and [[Skill Check|skill checks]]. 
The curse can only be removed by completing the task, or if you release them from the curse by wiping off the mark that appears.
<span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">Confidant</span> [[charm]] - You curse a creature to trust you (will negates). While cursed, they are willing to divulge their deepest secrets to you while in private, as though you were a close friend, family member, lover or religious authority. It is a simple matter to convince cursed individuals into having these conversations, and also to steer the direction in one you find desirable.
A third party must insult the caster in your presence, you insult the cursed individual, or suitable evidence is presented to the cursed individual that you shared their secrets are ways to remove the curse.
## <span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">1 - Cauldron</span>
You get the [[alchemy]] skill, with a +4 bonus. You can use alchemy instead of [[+Survival]] in order to [[extract resources]].
You can also turn a mundane piece of cooking equipment into a site for a magical ritual. This requires at least 2 hours of cooking before you can produce anything of value. You can only use items harvested using the Extract Resources function of survival in a cauldron concoction. Each potion requires you spend at least 1 harvested resource and [[Crystallized Mana]] equal to the [[LvL]] of the creature involved in making the potion.

You start off with the ability to make two concoctions...
<span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">Shapeshifter Potion</span> [[Sp]] [[glamer]] - You can create a potion that, when consumed, visibly transforms the consumer into the creature type used in the potions ingredients. You don't transform into an exact copy of the creature, but can be mistaken for a family member. You gain none of its abilities, size or speed, though other creatures will still treat you as though you were the creature's size. Effectively though you are standing in the center of a giant illusion that bends to your will. The potion lasts [[Caster Level|CL]] hours, but can be ended early by the consumer by vomiting the potion out.

## <span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">2 - Gifts</span> [[subtle]]
Instead of casting a curse on a creature directly, you can instead infuse a handheld item with a curse. If a creature accepts your gift, then they are automatically afflicted with the curse with no saving throw. This usually makes it very obvious the source of the curse if the effects are immediate, like [[Death Mark]], but the gifts become much more useful for spells like [[Forest's Curse]].

In order for the cursed item to take effect, it needs to be accepted willingly in an exchange or otherwise, and cannot be simply slipped into someone's pocket. Once accepted, the cursed item continues affecting the creature forever, until it is destroyed. Even the usual methods of removing the curse are ineffective while the gift persists, and the gift cannot be regifted. Regardless of the type of item it is, the magic within it can be safely removed by burning it in a fire. The DC to [[Identify Affliction or Curse (P)|identify curse]] is 5 higher than its usual DC in order to figure out the cursed item plays a role.
