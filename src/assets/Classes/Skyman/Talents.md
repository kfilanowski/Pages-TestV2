## <span style="color:rgb(0, 176, 80)">Combat Talents</span>

<span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">Potion Chugger</span> - You can drink any [[+Potions|potions]] as a free action 1/turn.

<span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">Staggering Shield</span> [[Recharge]] - Your shield attacks stagger [[On-hit]] (fort negates). A creature can only be subject to this effect once a minute.
<span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">Ker-plink</span> - When you take the [[Shields Up]] action against a [[Ranged Attack|Ranged]] [[Weapon Attack]], you (or the target you shield) negate its damage entirely. This only functions for 1 attack with shields up and doesn't work against [[+Siege Weapon|siege weapons]].
<span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">Bowl 'em Over</span> - You gain the [[Trample]] ability while you wield a shield, dealing 2d6+[[LvL]] crush damage.

<span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">Paralyzed</span> - As an instant action when you would take [[Damage]] or suffer a [[Status|status effect]], you can instead choose to [[Stunned|stun]] yourself until the end of your next turn.
<span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">Face Tank</span> [[Healing]] - Whenever you provoke an [[AoO]], gain 10 [[Temp HP]].

<span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">Just Your Imagination</span> - Gain the [[Sniper]] feat. If you successfully snipe an enemy to ambush and the enemy fails their [[+Perception]] check, combat does not start.

<span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">Buy One, Get One Free</span> - Gain the [[Bonded Weapon]] feat. If you bind to a light or one-handed weapon, you get an exact copy of that weapon, including all materials, [[Equipment and Economics/Equipment Types/Gear Upgrades/Magic Weapon Properties/+Magic Weapon Properties|magic weapon properties]], etc. One of them is always considered the original, and if it is taken from you for longer than 1 minute, the copy disappears.

<span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">Gift of Giving</span> - You can use [[Thievery]] to place a [[+Toxins|Toxin]] on an unaware creature's person somewhere (the skyman is very creative. Not even creatures without pockets are safe).  At any point before the creature realizes it’s been planted and disposes of it, the Skyman may activate the poison as a free action, as though fulfilling whatever the usual delivery method is.

<span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">(10) Bucket</span> [[Su]] - You can [[Mount]] a common bucket, giving you a [[Fly]] speed equal to your [[land speed]]. If you are ever [[Poise]] broken, or otherwise separated from your bucket, you plummet.

<span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">(4) Horse Technology (Su)</span> [[Teleportation]] - Gain the [[Pet]] feat, and the creature must be a horse. With a whistle (free action), you can teleport this horse by your side. If this horse dies, it is banished to the horse dimension and you cannot summon it again until you rest.
Your horse also shares your Blessing of the Mountain Goat feature.

<span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">(4) Combat Caster</span> [[Sp]] - Choose an elemental [[Damage]] tag. You permanently memorize level 1 spell with that elemental damage tag. 
Gain a [[Multiattack]]:
Cast a level 1 spell with your chosen elemental damage tag and make a [[Melee Attack]].
<span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">Combat Medic</span> [[Sp]] - You permanently memorize level 1 spell with the [[Healing]] tag.
Gain a [[Multiattack]]:
Cast a level 1 spell with the healing tag and make a [[Melee Attack]].
<span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">Martial Prowess</span> - Gain a [[Multiattack]]:
Make 2 attacks.

<span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">(4) Housecarl</span> [[Companion]] - Gain the assistance of a basic companion. They are a [[human]] [[fighter]] with no archetype, no alternate racial features and only combat feats. 
- Their [[LvL|Level]] is your level-3
- They have starting stats of [[STR|STR]] +3, [[DEX]] +1, [[LUK]] +0, [[INT]] +0, [[WIS]] +0, [[CHA]] +0
If your housecarl dies, you must pay either their family or their employer 5,000g before you can acquire a new one.

## <span style="color:rgb(0, 176, 80)">Utility Talents</span>

<span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">Courier </span>- You have a loyal squad of three couriers that always appear at your beck and call. They can carry up to 10 pounds and will always reach their destination within one day. These couriers know the safest routes and generally refuse to go to particularly dangerous locations. If killed, get a letter from a replacement telling you about the last one’s death within 24 hours and all of your couriers take a 2 week vacation.

<span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">Fast Travel (teleportation) (recharge)</span> - 1/week you can teleport to any [[Teleportation Circles]]. This ritual costs 200g of a rare white chalk and 1 minute to perform. You can bring up to 8 other creatures, provided they are no larger than large size.

<span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">Roaming Alchemist</span> - You can find alchemy crafting materials in all manner of places. While travelling out and about in the world, you can generate materials as though using your [[Alchemy]] skill as a profession, but it is only half as effective.

<span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">Master Enchanter </span>- You gain an additional skill proficiency that must be spent on a crafting [[+Profession]]. You gain an additional underwear slot that can function as an additional item slot for any type of item, but it cannot be [[Armor|medium armor]] or [[Armor|heavy armor]].

<span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">Just Checking</span> - You can freely check a creature's pockets, bags etc to see what they are carrying on their person without scrutiny. Stealing it still requires [[Thievery]].

<span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">I Belong Here</span> - All creatures of your level or lower treat you as though you belong where you are.
You can walk into people's homes uninvited, and for no reason. You can exist within a royal palace like you lived there. NPCs do the mental gymnastics required to justify you existing wherever you are. There are some upper limits when it comes to interacting with NPCs, like sleeping in their beds.
You can do anything you like, so long as it does not break any major laws, like theft, assault etc. Even still, if nobody notices you do these things, who can say it was you?

<span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">Ragdoll</span> - You can appear dead as a standard action, rendering yourself [[Helpless]], but still aware of your surroundings. Only a [[+Medicine]] check (DC 20+[[LvL]]) can determine that you’re faking it.  When you “die,” you can set a condition (I hear the word bazinga) or a duration to awaken.

<span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">Speech 100</span> - Gain the [[+Persuasion]] skill. You can use it to [[Gather Information]] in d4 minutes. You can automatically end a conversation with an NPC with no repercussions, as though the conversation had ended naturally.
In addition, you can initiate conversations with any NPC, provided they aren't already in the middle of a conversation or in combat. No other situations are valid for denying conversation with you. For whatever reason, you seem as though you have the qualifications and social standing to interact with just about anyone. Creatures do not treat you well necessarily, but they will hear what you have to say. Once the conversation comes to a natural end, you cannot force your way into a conversation for another 24 hours.



## [[Crust]]
Fast Travel?
You can make it to any destination in the world in 3 days flat. The location you decided in cannot be a specific latitude and longitude coordinate and must therefore have some kind of name or significance, such as a settlement or a natural wonder. If the location of this place is widely unknown, and your group is not an exception, you cannot travel to it.
