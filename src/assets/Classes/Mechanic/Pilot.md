**1 - Mech Basics**
Mechs must be one size category larger than you to use. In order for another creature to use your mech, they must roll a DC (20+your engineering bonus) every round.

You are treated as Mounted on your mech, but it uses your statistics. Any damage it takes depletes your HP and any effects targeting the mech target you (such as Charming Gaze). It is not a separate creature, but an extension of yourself. It needs repair when you are [[Defeated]] while mounted, but in exchange, your Defeat condition changes. As long as you remain inside, you are treated as being at 1 HP, will stop being Defeated after one minute of not taking damage, and may then exit safely to start repairs. If you take enough damage to die outright, the Mech explodes, killing you with it.

While piloting, your own arms are occupied. You use the Mech's arms instead. By default, your mech has two arms and two legs. Its arms can perform [[Unarmed]] attacks, but also Pincer [[Natural Weapon|Natural Weapons]]. 
Pincer: 2d6 [[Crush]] Damage, [[AS]] 2, Close Natural Weapon.
The Mech may change what its arms are at the start of its turn, similarly and simultaneously to [[Switching Weapons]]. Its arms are part of its body, and thus cannot be [[Disarm|Disarmed]] like a regular weapon can.

A Mech weighs about a ton. You can build spare sets of parts ahead of time (in the time it would normally take to make a Mech) to quickly replace a lost Mech in the time it would normally take to repair one.

**1 - Emergency Exit**
[[Recharge]]. At any point, you can eject from your mech as an [[Instant Action]] and appear in its space. Remounting requires a [[Swift Action]].
