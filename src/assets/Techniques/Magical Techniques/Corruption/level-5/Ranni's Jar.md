Level 5 [[Corruption]]
Tags: [[Curse]]
Action: Standard
Focus: A mundane glass jar

| Range          | 30 ft      |
| -------------- | ---------- |
| Target/[[AoE]] | 1 creature |
| Duration       | -          |
| Save           | Will       |
This is a spell often used to capture people and keep prisoners. You pop open the jar and you suck a creature into it. They are shrunk down to diminutive size and imprisoned in the jar (will reduces to [[Sickened]] 1). The imprisoned creature has no ability to escape the jar via any kind of magic (including [[Teleportation]]) or attacking the jar from the inside. Any such effects automatically [[Fizzle|fizzles]]. They can still take any other kind of actions they wish (to heal, buff or communicate their imprisonment) but through no action of their own can they escape.

While the creature inside has no way to escape, all it takes for them to return to their usual size and exact vengeance is to destroy the jar. The jar cannot be fortified to increase its durability, magically or otherwise. As such, it is easily broken. If the jar is unattended, any attack will suffice. If it is being held on someone's person, you must use [[+Sleight of Hand]] to steal it or [[Poise]] break the creature to knock it free and cause it to shatter.

There are a handful of conveniences that make keeping the creature prisoner convenient. The top to the jar only functions for the creature kept inside, allowing the captor to slip in objects such as food or water. This also conveniently lets the creature breathe. The jar only muffles the sounds made from the inside, so special measures would need to be taken for rowdy or supernaturally loud prisoners.
