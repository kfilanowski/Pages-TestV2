<span style="color:rgb(192, 0, 0)">Note</span> - 
You must have 2 free hands to initiate this technique (unless already grappling, then just 1).
Against larger creatures, this requires [[Carry Weight|extraordinary strength]]

Make a [[grapple]] attack against a creature with a +4 bonus. If successful, immediately start spinning them around like they are a weapon. If you were already grappling the creature, you can benefit from this immediately, requiring no additional grapple attack.

Your target immediately becomes [[sickened]] 2. Until the start of your next turn, you can make [[Melee Attack|melee attacks]] using this creature. Functions as 2h [[Improvised Weapons|improvised weapon]] (granting 2d8 base damage) and an [[Unarmed Attack|unarmed strike]] for expanded synergy. You make a [[simple attack]] immediately when you succeed in initiating this grapple (or when you already started grappling). Each attack that you make automatically deals damage to the grappled creature as though you had hit them, even if you miss. If you [[crit]], then you crit the grappled creature as well. If you are still grappling the creature by the start of your next turn, your wild swinging ends and they are made sickened 2 again.



<span style="color:rgb(192, 0, 0)">Note 2 - </span>
Making something "count as x" is always weird. Does it work with z? The main thing you should as is does it work with x OR y. It always functions using the most advantageous features of both (ie how unarmed strikes come with the [[Finesse]] property).
