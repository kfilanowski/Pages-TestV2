Combat Maneuvers are attacks that target [[Poise]]. When making a Combat Maneuver, you must decide which specific form it takes, such as a [[Topple]], [[Dirty Trick]], [[Shove]] etc. A successful Combat Maneuver will inflict a condition based on the form you chose. 

You can combat maneuver an ally automatically. This allows you do things such as to [[Drag]] or [[shove]] an ally out of danger, but it sets their poise to 1.

You can apply bonuses to combat maneuvers to any maneuver you make, even if it doesn't originate from you directly. This means when you successfully shove a creature with [[Grasping Corpse]] and you have the [[Maneuver Master]] feat, it [[AoO|Provokes]] from all your allies. The one exception to this rule is effects that make [[Companion|companions]]. Since they are their own creatures, they don't have your features.
