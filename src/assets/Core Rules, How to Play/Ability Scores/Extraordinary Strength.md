There are numerous ways to increase your ability to lift and break things in the game. In order to make that investment seem valuable and to maintain thematic cohesion, many abilities that allow you to throw your opponent easily have this "extraordinary strength clause." Without it, you might get fairies able to toss around giants. I know that not all creatures of the same size are the same weight, but this is an abstraction to get a sense of where, ballpark, you should be in order to start thinking about flipping giant monsters like they are pancakes.

| <span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">Creature Size</span> | <span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">Carry Weight</span> | <span style="color:rgb(255, 192, 0)">STR Bypass</span> |
| --------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| Medium                                                    | ~100                                                     | +0                                                     |
| Large                                                     | ~400                                                     | +3                                                     |
| Huge                                                      | ~1,600                                                   | +4                                                     |
| Massive                                                   | ~6,400+                                                  | +4                                                     |
Basically, if your STR or carryweight matches or exceeds these numbers, go wild. If not, then tossing creatures shouldn't be something you can do.

- Race features (like [[Amazon]]'s [[Titanic Lift]])
