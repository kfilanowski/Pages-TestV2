In media it's very common for creatures to have minions, squires and sidekicks. This is what the companion system is for. Like most other content, the companion system is ill-suited for large quantities of creatures, like armies. It's meant to be more compact. Something to take with you on the go for your adventure. This could also reasonably extend to temporary allies that join your party.

Companions are frequently tied to your class, abilities and items. Any such ability is marked with this tag.

If an ability has the companion tag, it summons an ally, or otherwise serves some kind of interpersonal function.

## <span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">Companions and Defeat</span>
What happens to your companions when you are defeated?
- All your companions lose your CHA to attack
- Your companions lose any [[+Teamwork Feats|teamwork feats]] you were giving them.
- If your companion was created by some type of magical effect, such as a [[Summoning Contract]] or a [[Mud Buddy]], then it disappears immediately when you are defeated.
- If your companion exists outside of you and your magic (such as a squire or [[Beast Companion]]) then it does not disappear.
- At GM discretion, lesser companions (like [[Minion|minions]]) may even flee when their leader is defeated
## <span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">More rules for companions</span>
- You can only have 2 companions, or 1 if you have -1 [[CHA]].
- Companions always act on your turn if you are present. There is no separation between your turns, you all act in the same [[Initiative]]. 
- Companions gain bonuses or penalties based off of your CHA.
- If you are not currently sharing your [[+Teamwork Feats|teamwork feat]] gained through CHA with anybody, or you hate them and like your [[Snow Man]] better, you can grant it to a companion that you summon as a free action as part of creating them.
- If a feature generates a specific type of companion, like a [[Beast Companion]] or [[Mud Buddy]], then you cannot have another companion of the same type unless otherwise specified.
- If a companion is generated temporarily (such as through a technique or a Su ability), then it has a "leash" range of 120 ft. In addition, you need line of sight to your companion. The first turn it exists beyond this range it slowly starts to fade away. At the end of the second it disappears.

## <span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">Acquiring companions outside of class</span>
As stated previously, companions are most frequently obtained by summoning them via spells or as core class features. That does not mean this is necessary however. If for story reasons you encounter characters willing to follow you, and they are [[Minion|minions]] relative to you, you can take them under your wing and give them companion status. If they later gain levels such that they are no longer a minion, they no longer qualify for being a companion.
