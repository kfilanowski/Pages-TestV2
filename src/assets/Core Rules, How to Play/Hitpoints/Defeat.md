When you take damage that reduces you to zero hp, you are [[defeated]], and must be healed to regain consciousness in a timely manner.

You can choose to non-lethally defeat someone, even retroactively after the battle, causing them to survive the encounter (if they don't receive further damage). If this is not the case, they must make a DC 8 [[luck check]] or <span style="color:rgb(192, 0, 0)">die</span>. Creatures that manage to survive being defeated have to keep making this luck check every time they take damage while defeated.

<span style="color:rgb(192, 0, 0)">When an enemy healer is present</span>, or other healing options like potions are present, players must be more considerate of how they approach the situation. The GM should consider what the players usually prefer when fighting, mercy or death, or should directly ask players if they want to shoot to kill to avoid situations where the players claim "actually I killed them" once a healer arrives on the scene.

When a character recovers from the defeated condition, they are [[dazed]] on their next turn.

When recovering from the defeated condition, you also gain an [[Affliction]].

When a [[Main Character Status|main character]] (ie usually a player) falls to zero HP, they are not at risk of instant death. Instead of rolling luck checks, a main character gets a stack of [[Doom]] when they take damage while defeated. Upon acquiring the 5th stack, they die. Doom stacks are lost on rest.

See [[Recovering from Defeat]] for what happens when defeated creatures are left to their own devices.
