The default [[Land Speed]] of the game is 30, which translates to about 3.4 mph. Assuming the average day is spent sleeping for 10 hours (on shifts with someone keeping watch), walking for 10 hours and eating/taking breaks/setting up camp for the rest, the average person can walk 34 miles per day. This is what I will assume when making the following chart where I do the same process but for creatures with higher/lower speed.

Keep in mind, if one member of your party is quite fast, unless they can carry the others, they are slowed down by the rest of the party. Overland travel is determined by the slowest member. This is why many use [[rocks]] or [[teleportation circles]] to travel in Nack.

You can also choose to <span style="color:rgb(192, 0, 0)">hustle</span> in order to double your daily distance, but you are [[exhausted]] the following day. Doing this for multiple days in a row without [[rest]] inflicts [[Drained|drain]]. You can also choose to <span style="color:rgb(192, 0, 0)">rush</span> in order to move distance in 2 hours, after which you are [[exhausted]]. This is mostly for getting to important nearby locations when you're on a timer.

<span style="color:rgb(192, 0, 0)">Note:</span> For reference, in ideal conditions (ie safe and well maintained roads) [[Rocks]] can move 600 miles each day, or 1,000 if left on autopilot overnight.


## <span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">Using Exhaustion</span>
[[Exhausted|Exhaustion]] is a stacking debuff that is intended to be the primary method you inflict general debuffs on the players when travelling through treacherous terrain, hustle and rush during overland travel or when they refuse to [[rest]].

Some of these things mention how they inflict exhaustion, but others don't. When they don't...
1. Try and think of a time frame (every time they do x, or every y days). 
2. Then have all characters roll [[Fortitude]] saves, deciding on a DC based on conditions (usually somewhere around 10-15). Characters that fail are exhausted.
3. Give characters that prepare effectively bonuses, like [[Advantage|adv]] for bringing copious amounts of water into deserts, or +2 for having a thick coat for the cold. Some factors may eliminate the need for a roll entirely. 
4. For every time they roll one of these saves, increase the DC by a few points.
