Players are expected to go on adventures, but adventures don't always happen back to back. What happens in between?

## <span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">Campaign Pacing</span>
Because downtime can often lead to long periods of time passing by relative to how long adventures take, its worth considering how these activities fit into the pace of your campaign. Most of the math regarding activities that happen over time is measured in weeks, so that will be the basic assumption in most things listed here. If your campaign has a months or years of time skips, players suddenly having 10s of thousands of gold from [[+Profession]] can give them access to powerful items earlier than expected. On the flip side, if the structure of your campaign has rapid fire adventures with no downtime at all, players might not get the chance to do any of these activities, and how you award gold/items should be adjusted accordingly.

If you don't want to adjust these systems, then don't use them. 

## <span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)">Downtime</span> 
Things like earnings from profession are generally measured in weeks. Using the [[+Medicine]] skill to<span style="color:rgb(103, 254, 120)"></span> provide [[Long Term Care (P)|long term care]] can a long time, depending on how wounded the individual is. Using these types of activities as a framework, I would also like to add other types of activities that you can fill your time with if you aren't a skilled laborer. 

Over time I will add more things here. This is what I have in mind. I will also include how repeatable I think they should be and, how long they should take and other details.
<span style="color:rgb(192, 0, 0)">Acquiring entirely new</span> [[Skills]] 
- ~3 months at more or less depending on the skill
- Not repeatable or limited
- Requires teacher, the skill of which can change how quickly you learn
<span style="color:rgb(192, 0, 0)">Learning a</span> [[Languages|language]] 
- For working proficiency, maybe 2 weeks. 
- For mastery, ~4 weeks
<span style="color:rgb(192, 0, 0)">Creating a new</span> [[Technique Rules|technique]]
- Few months for a low level technique, many months for a high level technique
- Large quantities of research materials, including money and a physical location fit for the task
- Some form of inspiration, be it lost arts, copying something you fought, or meeting a very specific need
<span style="color:rgb(192, 0, 0)">Research</span>
- Time varies wildly based on how esoteric the knowledge is and where your sources of information are coming from. 
- A clear goal, like "discover how Bane the dragon keeps coming back to life after we kill her"
- Large quantities of research materials, including either the money to purchase them or a physical location fit for the task (library, church, knowledgeable oracle)
<span style="color:rgb(192, 0, 0)">Crafting Items</span>
- Using the [[+Profession]] skill or [[Alchemy]], create things
- Varies wildly based on price
- Some type of raw material related to what you are trying to create
- Mana (see [[Mana Donation]])
- Other crafting details TBD, but these are the basics

- Change one of your [[Feat Functionality|feats]]
