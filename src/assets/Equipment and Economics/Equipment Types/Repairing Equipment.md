While there aren't any explicit rules that allow you to break equipment, to maintain realism, some circumstances can cause your equipment to break. This could be using your sword to cut down a tree and lose its edge, or using a [[+Shield|shield]] to block a [[+Siege Weapon]].

When equipment is <span style="color:rgb(192, 0, 0)">broken</span>, but not completely destroyed, it becomes unusable for its intended purpose. Any passive abilities (like a shield's [[Poise]]) or active abilities (like a sword's [[Base Special Attacks|cleave]]) for broken equipment do not function. It requires 10% of the items value in appropriate materials and an appropriate skill for repairing said item (usually [[+Profession]]). This takes however long it would take someone with that skill to make that much money (see [[Economics]] for more details).

When an item is <span style="color:rgb(192, 0, 0)">destroyed</span>, then it cannot be repaired through traditional means. If it can be repaired at all, it would require special crafting components and a skilled craftsman.
