| Appearance and Biology                                | Drakonids are bulky humanoids with draconic heads, tails, teeth and scales. Their colorful scales cover nearly their entire bodies and give them a very regal look. Unlike true dragons, they do not possess wings. Other features remain that leave a lot to be desired when compared to true dragons, such as their large claws, and long muscular necks that allow them to effectively make use of their sharp teeth. In this sense, they more closely resemble dinosaurs.<br><br>They inherit many aspects of reptilian biology, including their ability to lay eggs. Drakonid babies skip the helpless infant stage of development most other humanoids experience, able to crawl around immediately after hatching so they can get to wasting furniture with their breath weapon.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| Warrior Culture                                       | The Drakonids reintroduced to the world were the children of [[Niv the Protector]]. After her adventures ended, her work continued, and still does continue to this day. Her family is the first of a handful to gain a significant cultural foothold, doing so through their services as protectors to travelers through the [[Land of Earth]]. As such, if someone has heard of a Drakonid, they are likely martials clad in metal wielding weapons of war.<br><br>In general, Drakonid work to ensure order in society and safety to citizens. As such, the wanderer or adventurer type Drakonids people find tend to have poor family relations.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| Minions                                               | Once [[Gralth the Inferno]] reintroduced Drakonids to the world, many other powerful dragons took notice. They to wanted to have envoys into humanoid society, but they wanted to maintain their loyalty. In doing so they created a different variation of Drakonid that is much smaller and much more obedient. These smaller Drakonid are referred to as "minions" due to their propensity to follow a more powerful true dragon.<br><br>The servile nature of these creatures often makes other races have lowly opinions of minions, and as such, they tend to do poorly when it comes to diplomacy. | History of Dragons                                    | Drakonids are a race that fall in and out of popularity depending on when in [[+The Timeline]] you play in. They even have different names. During the [[The Age of Dragons]], dragons were cursed to be nothing more than giant flying lizards by [[Drakthar, the World Ender]], who consumed their power in order to fuel his own.<br><br>Once Drakthar was defeated, his power was restored to its natural place and was once again allowed to suffuse the world. In particular, the band of dragons that defeated him became the first true dragons: the 4 legged quadrapeds with large wings jutting from their back. These progenitors made it their mission to spread the love so to speak, and granted all draconic creatures sentience. <br>Many other dragons had different evolutionary paths, such as the wyverns with winged arms, or serpentine dragons that used magic to fly. One group was bipedal and these were later called Proto Dragons. They didn't have nearly the same offensive profile as other dragons, tending to be much smaller and many being unable to fly at all. <br><br>Over time, many groups of humanoids hunted down dragons. Part of it was the hundreds of years of animosity, but part of it was wanting to steal their power. In particular, tribes of elves and dwarves that were once forced to live deep underground used this time in history to become powerful civilizations off the backs of the mana and materials they hunted from dragons.<br><br>One such group was the Proto Dragons. Already small in number, many were captured and sealed away using powerful curse magic so their mana could be harvested for generations. For many centuries, humanoid dragons all but disappeared. It was not until [[Gralth the Inferno]] finally reintroduced the species during the [[The Age of Magic]], with the hopes of using the species as a way to improve dragon-humanoid relations. In the decades since, the species has been allowed to flourish, mostly forming tight family groups in [[Land of Earth]]. |
